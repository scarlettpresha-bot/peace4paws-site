In Loving Memory

Rescue is built by people who give their time, heart, and care to animals in need. Their compassion remains part of Peace4Paws and continues to inspire the work we do today.

Transporter & Handler

Kyle McSweeney

Forever part of the Peace4Paws family

Kyle was the volunteer who almost always said yes — driving across state lines, in all kinds of weather, to bring dogs out of danger and home to safety.

Read Kyle's story ▾

Peace4Paws volunteer Kyle Christopher McSweeney, aged 37, of Norwood, NJ, passed away suddenly. He was an employee of the State of New Jersey as a Taxation Investigator for 14 years, a 2010 graduate of Quinnipiac University, and a 2006 graduate of Northern Valley Regional High School in Old Tappan. An accomplished wrestler from the age of seven, he won numerous tournaments and ultimately won the State Wrestling Tournament as a freshman, inspiring both of his brothers to take up the sport.

Kyle loved spending time at the beach and driving his car wherever the road would take him. He loved music and had played guitar since he was young. He met his favorite band, 311, whom he had loved since he was ten, and formed a long-term friendship with the band’s bassist, P-Nut — even playing backstage with him at Hammerstein Theater in New York City at seventeen. A devoted Dallas Cowboys fan since childhood, he never lost hope in his BOYZ.

On weekends and sometimes during the week, Kyle volunteered for Peace4Paws as an extremely reliable transporter and handler of dogs. He would routinely drive out to Matamoras, PA on a Sunday and bring his dogs to Best Friends in Chestnut Ridge, New York. No matter how big or small, scared or bold the dog, Kyle was committed to handling it until the foster, adopter, or boarding time came. Sometimes he was part of a team of six drivers filled with carloads of fully vetted, precious pups being saved from euthanasia. He knew the gravity of his work and was always patient and sweet with the dogs.

Kyle pretty much always said yes when asked to transport. He picked up giant pit bulls in New York City and delivered senior poodles all the way to Maryland and back in a single day. On an urgent, last-minute request he drove to Wheeling, West Virginia and brought back a beautiful white pit bull to be adopted in Washington Township, NJ — also in one day — and loved the ride through the mountains of Pennsylvania and West Virginia. He even helped founder Jennifer Cece when she had post-concussion syndrome, driving her to the Bronx for an opera rehearsal.

In February 2022, as a new member, he was recognized at our fundraiser and awarded a service plaque even though he had volunteered less than a year — an exception we made because Kyle was simply that special to us. He always showed up with a smile and was a joy to have on the team.

“There really are no words to express the depth of sadness for losing this wonderful young man. We were so fortunate for the years we spent working with Kyle. He always brightened up every day he spent in service to the dogs. He will be missed.” — Jennifer Cece

In lieu of flowers, Kyle’s family asked that donations be made to Peace4Paws in his memory. Our thoughts remain with his family and friends, who lost a wonderful young man far too soon. He will be forever in our hearts.

Volunteer, Handler & Trainer

Anthony Sanderson

An angel on earth · his mission continues

An angel on earth — a gifted young trainer who saved countless lives in a short time, and whose mission continues through the scholarship fund created in his name.

Read Anthony's story ▾

Our beloved volunteer, dog walker, and dog trainer Anthony Jason Sanderson, of Nutley, NJ, formerly of Harrison, NJ, passed away peacefully in his sleep early Sunday morning, August 21st, 2022. He was just three days away from turning twenty, and just a few weeks away from completing an online and hands-on dog training program.

In a relatively short but dynamic time, Anthony amassed hundreds of hours of volunteer service — fostering, pet-sitting, dog walking, and training under various mentors, including Jerome Watson, for all kinds of Peace4Paws dogs, especially the challenging ones. He was such a talented young man that, starting a year before, we were able to refer him as a trainer and hire him ourselves, just as we did other adult trainers. He handled so many necessary and urgent situations in a timely manner — and in rescue, “timely” usually means immediately. He never said no. Anthony saved so many lives.

As he grew mature enough to head into New York City alone for special pit bull assignments, he carried them out with enthusiasm and reliability. He managed and trained dogs that few, if any, other people could handle: dogs with serious medical problems like advanced heart murmurs or heartworm, dogs with difficult behavioral issues, aggressive dogs, skittish and scared dogs, and mouthy large-breed pit bulls. He worked with Veronica Tejado’s dog Lucy of Jersey City, who used to bark at every dog and person around — with Anthony’s regular help, she stabilized into a sweet, well-behaved dog. Patiently and gently, he gained their trust, taught them the behaviors that made them adoptable, and showed their owners how to best guide them too.

Anthony loved being part of our community, and he truly loved working with dogs. He always had such a positive aura, full of love and enthusiasm. Every time he showed up, we all felt a sense of relief, reassured by the thought that “Anthony is here” or “Anthony is handling it.” His passing is an absolutely huge loss to his family, to Peace4Paws, and to the world. His mom shared that once he started volunteering for Peace4Paws, his grades improved and his confidence grew exponentially.

Because he was intuitive, smart, compassionate, and committed, his legacy must live on.

The Anthony Sanderson Dog Training Scholarship Fund was created in his honor to support volunteers who have demonstrated service and commitment, so they can continue their education and Anthony’s mission of caring for dogs goes on. If you are unable to contribute financially, Anthony would have loved nothing more than for you — or your child — to volunteer to walk, care for, groom, foster, or adopt a dog in need. We ask you to honor his legacy.
